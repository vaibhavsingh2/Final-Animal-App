The term 'Species of Concern' is an informal designation used by a region to identify species that may require concentrated conservation efforts. The specific conservation actions undertaken vary according to the health of the populations and the types and levels of threats faced. At one end of the spectrum, these actions may entail periodic monitoring of species populations and associated threats to their habitats. At the other end, a species may warrant designation as a federally recognized threatened or endangered species. It is essential to note that species labeled as 'Species of Concern' do not receive legal protection, and the use of this term does not necessarily indicate that a species will eventually be proposed for listing as threatened or endangered.
